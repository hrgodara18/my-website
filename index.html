India — A Tapestry of Culture, History & Flavour

From the snow-capped Himalayas to the tropical south, India is a land of contrasts — ancient traditions, bustling cities and breathtaking landscapes. Explore iconic landmarks, vibrant festivals, and culinary delights.

Start exploring

Overview

India is the world's largest democracy and one of the fastest-growing major economies. Its history spans millennia, shaped by empires, trade and cultural exchange. The country is home to diverse languages, religions and ecosystems.

Culture & Festivals

India's cultural fabric includes classical music and dance, literature in many languages, and a calendar full of festivals — Diwali, Holi, Eid, Christmas, Navratri and Pongal among others. Food, clothing and rituals vary widely by region.

A Brief History

Civilizations such as the Indus Valley flourished here thousands of years ago. Over centuries, empires like the Maurya, Gupta, Mughal and British Raj shaped the subcontinent, leaving a rich architectural and intellectual legacy.

Cuisine

Indian cuisine is famously regional — from spicy curries of the south and rich Mughlai dishes of the north to street-food cultures and coastal seafood. Must-try: masala dosa, biryani, butter chicken, chaat and regional sweets.

Top Places to Visit

Highlights include the Taj Mahal (Agra), Jaipur’s palaces, Kerala backwaters, Varanasi’s ghats, Ladakh’s high plains, Goa’s beaches and the Sundarbans mangroves. Each region offers unique landscapes and experiences.
